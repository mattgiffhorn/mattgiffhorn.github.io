Case Study: Amazon Halo Validation

Amazon partnered with our team to independently validate a computer vision algorithm designed to assess movement health using camera-based assessments. The goal was to enable consumers to measure movement quality at home and support scalable health insights within the Amazon Halo platform.

My Role

  • Study Coordinator & Clinical Lead
  • Assisted with protocol design
  • Led data collection operations
  • Managed cross-team coordination
  • Supported data cleaning and quality assurance

Collaborated with a 7-person internal research team and Amazon product partners.

Scope & Complexity

  • 150 participants enrolled
  • 8-month study duration
  • Lab-based validation designed for real-world deployment
  • Compared against motion capture, depth cameras, and clinical assessments

Each participant session required ~2 hours and a 3-person collection team.

Key Challenges

  • Translating clinical measures into product metrics
  • Depth camera data capture variability
  • Assessment ceiling effects
  • Recruitment logistics for diverse populations
  • Coordinating staffing across multiple concurrent projects

Cross-Functional Work

Worked directly with Amazon product partners, engineers, and data scientists. Weekly coordination ensured continuous alignment between clinical standards, technical requirements, and project timelines.

Results & Impact

  • Validated algorithm accuracy vs gold-standard assessments
  • Supported product readiness and feature launch
  • Demonstrated feasibility of scalable at-home movement health assessment
View publication

Strategic Takeaway

Successful healthcare AI deployment requires rigorous validation, cross-disciplinary alignment, and the ability to translate clinical standards into scalable product metrics. This project demonstrated how bridging those domains enables responsible and real-world ready health technology.

Case Study: Strong Strides (0→1 Program Build)

Strong Strides is a running analysis and performance program I created to expand service lines, differentiate the brand, and demonstrate a technology-forward clinical experience, without adding operational burden or requiring new equipment.

Context

At the time of launch, the organization had no structured running program, no running-focused technology stack, and limited partnerships with endurance events. Competitors offered “running analysis,” but often relied on slow-motion iPhone video or tools that required a separate follow-up visit and produced qualitative feedback rather than objective metrics.

I designed Strong Strides to provide real-time, objective biomechanical insights during the visit and to create a repeatable workflow clinicians could adopt across sites.

What I Owned

  • Concept → executive pitch → approval
  • Program design, assessment protocol, and visit structure
  • SOPs, onboarding, training, and resource libraries
  • Scheduling workflows + front desk enablement
  • Marketing positioning and referral pipeline build
  • Partnership development and ongoing program management
  • Scaling the launch across the entire company

Technology & Workflow Design

I selected Ochy as the motion analysis platform because it delivered real-time analysis and objective data without requiring additional equipment setup, helping minimize operational friction while improving consistency.

Ochy platform Program flyer

Business Goals

  • Differentiate brand with a technology-forward offering
  • Expand service lines and increase visit utilization
  • Attract new referral sources + diversify existing referral mix
  • Create a scalable program model deployable across sites

Implementation Challenges

  • Clinician buy-in: varying comfort with tech and running-related cases
  • Enablement: onboarding + training needed for confident delivery
  • Scheduling: front office workflow required a new appointment type
  • Integration: tying running goals into active plans of care
  • Billing: designed to fit within standard PT coding pathways

Results & Early Impact

  • 1,000+ running-related sessions company-wide within the first 6 months
  • Extended total visits by ~1–2 for existing clients with return-to-run goals
  • Enabled partnership growth (e.g., Mercy Home Chicago Marathon Team charity relationship)
  • Scaled adoption by developing 1–2 site champions per location
  • Drove ongoing word-of-mouth + provider referrals specific to the program

Strategic Takeaway

The most sustainable healthcare innovations are designed for workflow fit. Strong Strides succeeded because it paired compelling technology with operational simplicity: low equipment burden, clear training paths, scheduling alignment, and a repeatable model that could scale across sites without relying on a single expert.
